⸻

1. Reduce Overall Page Height & Scrolling
	•	Review all sections on the landing page and minimize vertical space:
	•	Reduce excessive top and bottom padding in large sections (hero, banners, gradients).
	•	Reduce the height of large gradient blocks and empty areas.
	•	Tighten spacing between sections, headings, and buttons.
	•	Target: on a typical laptop viewport, the full page should be accessible with no more than one or two scrolls.

⸻

2. Focus on Core Content & Remove Redundancy
	•	Identify duplicate or redundant payment/subscription blocks and:
	•	Merge them into a single concise pricing/plan section, OR
	•	Convert the secondary ones into small callouts instead of full-width blocks.
	•	Prioritize these elements above the fold and in the first scroll:
	1.	Clear hero section:
	•	Short headline (what CVPro does)
	•	One strong subheading (benefit)
	•	Primary CTA buttons: “Create CV / Get Started Free” and “Login”
	2.	Key benefits/features (3–5 bullet cards max).
	3.	A simple pricing preview or “Free vs Pro” teaser (not multiple repeated tags).
	•	Remove or compress any content that does not directly support:
	•	Understanding the product
	•	Encouraging signup/login
	•	Showing the value of the app

⸻

3. Improve Layout Structure
	•	Use a sectioned layout with clear blocks:
	•	Hero
	•	Key Features / Benefits
	•	Sample CV or screenshots (optional, compact)
	•	Pricing / subscription summary (single section)
	•	Footer
	•	Convert long text sections into:
	•	Short bullet lists
	•	2–3 column grids on desktop to reduce vertical height.
	•	Make sure important call-to-action buttons are visible in:
	•	Hero
	•	At least one place near the middle of the page.

⸻

4. Desktop & Mobile Behavior
	•	Optimize primarily for desktop first (since scrolling issue is most visible there), but:
	•	Ensure mobile still looks clean and not overly tall.
	•	Avoid huge vertical gaps on mobile.

⸻

5. Keep Existing Functionality Intact
	•	Do not change the login, signup, or payment logic.
	•	Only adjust layout, spacing, and content hierarchy.
	•	Make sure all existing links and CTAs still work after the redesign.

⸻

6. Visual Style
	•	Keep the current brand colors and overall style, but:
	•	Reduce oversized sections.
	•	Make the page feel more compact, modern, and focused.
	•	Ensure that when a new user lands, they immediately understand:
	•	What the platform does
	•	Why it is valuable
	•	How to start (login / get started button)
